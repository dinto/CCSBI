Institutional Biodiversity Assessment - Vallachira panchayath

The Institutional Biodiversity Assessment Program aims to map and understand the local biodiversity, with the goal of fostering a scientific mindset among the local population. The program collects data using the inaturalist platform, which allows for the documentation and mapping of biodiversity information. This platform helps people identify unknown species in the field and the recordings are automatically uploaded to a project within the Vallachira Panchayat boundary. The program has turned people like students, housewives, labourers and others into citizen scientists with the help of smartphones, promoting local biodiversity assessment.

The Vallachira Panchayat Biodiversity Documentation Program involved local stakeholders actively participating in the surveys. The survey was conducted in six groups, each led by an expert, covering different zones of the panchayat. The keen interest of volunteers helped to accelerate the documentation process. The documentation was done using smartphones and cameras, with photos taken on smartphones directly uploaded to the inaturalist app, and those taken with cameras uploaded later for identification.

The single-day survey conducted in the Vallachira Panchayat documented 77 bird species, 63 butterfly species, 28 dragonfly and damselfly species, 10 reptile species, 7 amphibian species, and 2 mammal species. Most species were identified using the inaturalist app, while some observations required further confirmation from experts within the platform. The use of citizen science tools sparked an interest in biodiversity documentation among the local population, leading to continued contributions of observation data and a steady influx of new information.
